Cicadra [シ・カ・デラ] - Insect Synthesizer

[2024] Synthesizer / Toys

Cicada-shaped synthesizer, inspired by the summer ambiance in Japan. The primary focus is on creating sounds that mimic the distinctive call of cicadas, the insects synonymous with Japanese summer. This workshop blends simple technology with artistic flair, offering a unique experience in crafting music inspired directly by nature.

This module was first developed for the Rokko Meets Art festival in Kobe, Japan. The "Persami" project, initiated by Waft Lab, serves as the main program encompassing various sub-workshops held at the event.

One of the modules within this project is Cicadra, a simple oscillator based on the NE555 IC circuit. Cicadra is designed with a straightforward approach to facilitate easy assembly, allowing workshop participants to easily grasp the fundamental principles of oscillation and sound experimentation.

With a design inspired by the cicada insect, this module not only functions as an experimental device for sound exploration but also serves as an interactive medium in the context of art and education.

If you're interested, you can assemble it yourself and experience firsthand the exploration of micro mecha cicada! Simply download the file below, print it, and follow the provided assembly instructions.

This module is designed to be accessible to everyone, whether you're a beginner or an experienced electronics geek. If you have any further questions about assembly, usage, or further exploration, feel free to contact me through the available channels.

Hybrid Player Cassette

[2024] Portable Audio Player

Hybrid Player Cassette is a portable digital audio player in the form of a cassette tape, developed by Awikworks as a multifunctional digital album. Powered by a 5V DC supply via a micro USB port, this device allows users to conveniently play audio content such as music albums or other audio files anywhere. Designed as a physical medium for a hybrid analog/digital album, the Hybrid Player Cassette offers a unique, personal, and collectible listening experience.

In this project, Awikworks collaborated with Nisrina Aulia (graphic designer), who designed the layout on the scratch paper material, and Urubingwaru (artist), who created a hand-painted cover illustration. This collaboration adds a strong visual element and transforms the Hybrid Player Cassette into a complete audio-visual artwork.

PKL [Penyintesis Kaki Lima / Sidewalk Synthesizer]

[2023] Modular Synthesizer

This is a representational piece resulting from the synthesis of integrated creations. As a synthesizer enthusiast, I often encounter various challenges in accessing it, particularly financial constraints. With unwavering determination, I embarked on creating synthesizer modules independently. I developed various synthesis modules by assembling or modifying various electronic devices. Many ideas and materials for my creations were sourced from the sidewalks. I compiled and arranged all my findings into a comprehensive synthesizer unit titled: Awikworks PKL (Penyintesis Kaki Lima >> Sidewalk Synthesizer).

This project was previously showcased at the Bebunyian Festival in Surabaya, an event that features a diverse range of performances and art installations, offering audiences an intimate encounter with unique sound-based mediums. The festival also provides opportunities for immersive experiences and public discussions, creating a space for deeper engagement with experimental audio-visual art. Hosted by the Korean Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism and organized by KOFICE (Korean Foundation for International Cultural Exchange), the event was curated by Baseline.

Bengox

[2023] Wearable Sound Reactive Light

Bengox is a collaborative project between Awikworks and Dongker, a punk band based in Bandung, West Java. This wearable piece merges DIY electronics with performance, featuring a sound-reactive module integrated directly into a balaclava mask. The module consists of a series of custom-built sound-responsive circuits and LED lights that animate in real time, reacting dynamically to the user's voice, whether speaking, shouting, or singing.

Each light pulse becomes a visual extension of sound and expression. "Bengox" is derived from the Javanese word Bengok, meaning “a loud scream,” reflecting both the raw energy of the punk spirit and the visceral nature of the piece itself. This project not only explores the intersection of music, fashion, and technology but also offers a striking commentary on voice, identity, and visibility in contemporary performance culture.

Kepiye Unine?

[2023] Synthesizer Module

Kepiye Unine is an experimental module developed around a simple yet curious idea: “What does it sound like?” or in Javanese: “Kepiye unine?”. This module was born out of a desire to uncover the hidden sounds within everyday objects around us.

Built using basic electronic components such as ICs, capacitors, resistors, and LEDs, Kepiye Unine allows users to explore sound in a fun and engaging way. Once assembled, users can attach its two pins to virtually any object—metal, plants, even the human body—and the module will respond by producing different sounds based on the unique characteristics of the object.

This project was developed at the Lifepatch Laboratory in Yogyakarta in mid-2023, as part of an interdisciplinary exploration between art, science, and technology. Kepiye Unine is not just a tool, it’s an invitation to explore the surrounding environment in a playful and curious way.

This module is designed to be accessible to everyone, whether you're a beginner or an experienced electronics geek. If you have any further questions about assembly, usage, or further exploration, feel free to contact me through the available channels.

MBOA PWM

[2023] DC Motor Speed Controller

MBOA PWM designed as a result of a collaboration between Awikworks, Lifepatch, and Stephane Fadanka, a molecular biologist from Cameroon. The module was developed during Stephane’s residency at Lifepatch in mid-June 2023.

The name “MBOA” is inspired by MboaLab, an open and collaborative space located in Yaoundé, Cameroon. MboaLab aims to promote sustainable local development and improve people’s quality of life through open science.

MBOA PWM is not just a technical device for controlling DC motor speed, it also stands as a symbol of cross-cultural collaboration and the spirit of friendship between two nations: Indonesia and Cameroon. This project demonstrates how science, technology, and cooperation can become a bridge connecting ideas, people, and hopes across borders.

Awikworks Origin.

[2017] The Four Horsemen of Awikworks Modulars

Starting from an interest in building a modular synthesizer independently, this project was initially named "Modular Miskin." However, over time, the name was felt to bring misfortune, leading to its change to "Awikworks" with the motto "low tech-high five." Equipped with open-source modules, Muzeian continued to develop his DIY synthesizer. In addition to purchasing electronic components directly, he often dismantled and modified various used electronic devices to be repurposed into new instruments.

From this project, a sub-project called "AMPRRRONG" was born. Through AMPRRRONG, he recorded an EP in 2020, released by Anti Trust Record under the title Salamander Kodok Amprong: BAB I – The Shell Creatures.. By 2023, the exploration of Awikworks Modular had entered its fourth stage of evolution, where several modules were assembled into a single compact unit.
